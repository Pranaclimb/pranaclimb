Breathwork Practices

Pranayama is always rhythmic. Breath control has an immediate impact on psychology, brain function, physiology and emotions. Even five minutes changes everything.

"Just one session of slow, deep breathing had beneficial effects on anxiety and vagal tone in adults. This may be likened to 'micro dosing' breathwork, similar to single session mindfulness meditation practices."

Meta-Analysis — Nature

🌊
Micro-Dosing Breathwork

5–10 minutes of deliberate breathing practice produces significant shifts in autonomic tone and well-being. Breathwork is rhythmic — varying in rate, depth, ratio, and intensity of inhales versus exhales.

🧠
Bi-Directional Attention

Breathing has a bi-directional relationship with attention. During exercises, attention to breathing removes attention from worldly worries. Sustain attention, handle distraction, spread attention — all through breath.

⚡
Immediate Impact

A breathing exercise proposing an equal inhalation/exhalation ratio promotes balance between sympathetic and parasympathetic activity, increases HRV, and promotes psychophysiological coherence.

6 Breaths Per Minute · 5s In / 5s Out

Diaphragmatic breathing at six breaths per minute with equal inhale and exhale ratios. Maximises HRV, increases VO2 Max, reduces anxiety, and builds cardiorespiratory fitness.

"Breathe in harmony — let your diaphragm set the tempo."

4-7-8 Breath
Dr. Andrew Weil · Deep Relaxation

Inhale 4 seconds, hold 7 seconds, forceful exhale 8 seconds. Powerful access to the relaxation response. Reduces anxiety, promotes sleep, and rapidly lowers physiological arousal.

"In 4 — hold 7 — release 8. Let the 8 carry everything away."

Kapalabhati
Skull Shining · Active Exhale / Passive Inhale

Rapid active exhalation by contracting the abdominal muscles — the inhale is passive. Cleanses the respiratory system, energizes the body, enhances lung capacity. Does not cause hyperventilation.

"Active out, passive in — let your belly do the work."

2:1 Extended Exhale
Reduce Fear · Heart Rate ↓

Taking twice as long to exhale as inhale slows the heart rate, decreases residual lung air, and reduces fear and anxiety. Try 4:2, 6:3, 8:4, 10:5, 12:6 — through the nose — for 2 minutes.

"Extend your exhale — think I'm OK."

Breathe in through nose, exhale slowly through tightly pursed or kiss lips. Opens airways during exhalation, improves gas exchange, relieves shortness of breath, and gains control when anxious.

"Kiss life — breathe your entire breath out."
