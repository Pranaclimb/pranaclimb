Zone-Based Coaching

Three Zones. One System.

Pranaclimb uses Breathing Rate (BR) + RPE to identify three practical performance zones. Every cue, every breath expression, every coaching decision maps back to these three zones.

Zone 1
BR <40

Recovery Zone

Below CP. RPE ≤6. W′bal is recharging.

  • Nasal breathing only
  • Slow, long exhales (1:2 or 1:3)
  • Relief sighs to reset parasympathetic
  • Loosen grip, shake out, relax shoulders
"Calm hands. Calm breath."
Zone 2
BR ~45–55

Grey Zone — CP Threshold

At CP → RCP. RPE 8–9. Small changes here decide the send.

  • Rhythmic 1:1 breathing
  • RP Sync: exhale to initiate movement
  • Micro-reset at BR >52: sigh + nasal + shake-out
"Exhale to move. Steady, not frantic."
Zone 3
BR >55

Crux Zone — Above RCP

Redline. RPE 9–10. Execute — then recover immediately.

  • Short, powerful exhales
  • One power exhale per crux maximum
  • No long breath holds — reset inhale ASAP
  • Return below CP as fast as possible
"Power exhale — reset immediately."

Zone Transitions — Where Good Coaching Lives

Zone 1 → Zone 2 BR rising toward 45 "Find rhythm before you push."
Zone 2 → Zone 3 BR >52–55 "Send — but breathe."
Zone 3 → Zone 2 Relief sighs + nasal control "Reset composure now."
Zone 2 → Zone 1 BR <45 + shaking out "Recharge — slow and deep."

Breath Cue Library

Named Cues for the Wall

Each cue is a memorable metaphor linked to a zone, a breath expression, and a coaching goal. Use them to connect physiology with what climbers actually feel.

🌬
Silk Breath
Zone 1 — Recovery

Smooth, effortless nasal inhale and exhale — almost silent. Minimises ventilation cost, keeps the nervous system calm.

"Breathe like silk — soft, seamless, flowing."
🌊
Tide Rhythm
Zone 1 — Recovery

4s nasal inhale, 6s nasal exhale — matched to the natural rise and fall of movement. Improves HRV, synchronises breath and body.

"Ride the tide — in and out, steady as the sea."
🐡
Puffafish Breath
Zone 1 — Reset

Inhale fully, puff cheeks, release slow forceful exhale through pursed lips. Relieves jaw and facial tension, resets rhythm after grunts or screams.

"Blow it out like a puffafish — slow, steady, let it go."
🎯
Anchor Breath
Zone 2 — Threshold

1–3 deliberate deep breaths to reset rhythm before a crux. Resets CO₂ drive, recentres pacing, improves efficiency.

"Anchor your breath — steady, reliable, unshaken."
🧭
Breath Compass
Zone 2 — Threshold

Direct breath down into the diaphragm and laterally into the ribs. North–south, east–west. Engages full lung capacity under strain.

"Let your breath be your compass."
🎼
Metronome
Zone 2 — Threshold

Inhale/exhale length matched to the timing of hand and foot placements. Keeps breathing rhythm linked to movement tempo, prevents overbreathing.

"Tick, tock — keep the breath on time."
⚡
Core Snap
Zone 3 — Crux

Short, forceful diaphragmatic exhale — "huh!" or "sshh!" — just before an explosive move. Increases intra-abdominal pressure, stabilises spine, sharpens motor output. (+3 BPM)

"Snap the breath — power from the core."
🗣
Power Exhale
Zone 3 — Crux

Strong vocalised exhale through pursed lips during a hard move. Enhances CO₂ clearance, narrows focus, boosts force. Use once per crux maximum. (+5 BPM)

"Exhale power — don't bottle it up."
🛡
Shield Breath
Zone 3 — Crux

Inhale 70–80% → brief 0.3–0.5s hold → short exhale under core brace. Combines core stability with controlled CO₂ clearance for high-force situations.

"Build your shield with the breath — strong, unbreakable."
🌬️
Exhale Dump
Recovery

Long, complete exhale with a sigh — "ahhh" — letting shoulders drop. Clears CO₂, releases muscular tension, signals recovery mode.

"Dump it out — start fresh."
🕰
HRR Reset
Recovery

Breathing at 5–6 breaths/min while tracking HR drop in the 60-second recovery window. Improves HRR₆₀ and parasympathetic dominance.

"Reset with the rhythm of your heart."
🌱
Grounding Breath
Recovery

Slow exhale while feeling feet press into rock or ground. Re-centres focus, activates baroreceptors for calm, lowers heart rate.

"Root into the rock — breathe through your base."

Quick Reference

Red Limiters & Quick Fixes

Red Limiter What It Means Quick Fix (Daily)
High BR at moderate effort Over-breathing below CP 5 min nasal-only warm-up + 1:2 extended exhale
Slow HRR₆₀ Poor between-attempt recovery 5–10 min coherent breathing at 5–6 BPM post-climb
Low PEFR Weak exhale strength 5× fast exhale bursts, twice daily
Low nasal use Early mouth-breathing Nasal-only warm-up climbs + 3 min alternate nostril breathing
Short CO₂ hold Early ventilatory drive 3× exhale-hold ladders + 5 min apnea walking
Poor rib mobility Restricted chest expansion 3× 1-min thoracic stretch-breathing blocks
