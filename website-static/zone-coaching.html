🪶Feather Breath

Soft, silent nasal inhale — imagine lifting a feather without disturbing it. Smooth, long exhale.

↓ fR, ↑ efficiency. Nasal NO supports perfusion. Stabilises BR in low–mid 30s.

"Breathe like silk — soft, seamless, flowing."

🌊Tide Rhythm

4s nasal inhale, 6s nasal exhale — match your breath to the rise and fall of waves.

Longer exhale ↑ vagal tone, optimises O₂ delivery, naturally reduces hidden breath-holds.

"Ride the tide — in and out, steady as the sea."

🔄Nasal Circuit

Continuous nasal breathing, no breaks between breaths (~10–16 BPM).

Keeps CO₂ tolerance high, encourages diaphragmatic expansion. Diagnostic: can't stay nasal here → efficiency or anxiety issue.

"Close the circuit — nose in, nose out."

🪞Mirror Breath

Imagine fogging and clearing a mirror with every exhale — slow, controlled, intentional.

Builds exhale control awareness; stabilises VT without fR spikes. Reduces unconscious breath-holds before movement.

"Mirror what you see — breathe with it."

🌱Grounding Breath

Slow, steady exhale paired with foot pressure awareness, feeling contact with the rock or ground.

Re-centres attention on contact points, activates baroreceptors for calm, lowers HR.

"Root into the rock — breathe through your base."

Red Limiter Pathway

Resting BR ≥ 18? Do 5 min Feather + Tide Rhythm before climbing, then keep Nasal Circuit on easy ground.

🎯Anchor Breath

1–3 deliberate deep breaths to reset rhythm before entering a crux or after a shake.

↓ cognitive noise, ↑ VT for a few moves. Resets CO₂ drive, recentres pacing.

"Anchor your breath — steady, reliable, unshaken."

🧭Breath Compass

Diaphragm down, ribs wide — breath spreading 360° around the lower torso (north-south, east-west).

Maximises tidal volume under strain, increases postural stability without Valsalva.

"Let your breath be your compass."

🧱Stacked Bricks

Layered rib expansion: low ribs → mid ribs → upper chest, as if stacking bricks evenly around the torso.

Increases tidal volume without increasing BR. Prevents involuntary shallow inhalations and micro breath-holds.

"Stack each breath like a brick — solid and even."

🎼Metronome Breathing

Inhale/exhale in sync with hand and foot placements (e.g. 2 moves/inhale, 3 moves/exhale).

Locomotor–respiratory coupling. Reduces decision noise, extends time at CP without tipping over.

"Tick, tock — keep the breath on time."

Depth cue: "Breathe in and make yourself as big as possible. Exhale and make yourself as small as possible." Pairs perfectly with Breath Compass and Stacked Bricks.

Red Limiter Pathway

BR spikes early near CP? Use Metronome Breathing + Stacked Bricks to hold VT without overbreathing.

💥Diaphragmatic Pop / Core Snap

Short, forceful diaphragmatic exhale ("huh!" or "sshh!") from the belly, then move.

↑ Intra-abdominal pressure → better power transfer, core stability. Replaces chaotic upper-chest panting.

"Snap the breath — power from the core."

🥷Silent Ninja Inhale

Keep inhale completely silent while exhaling softly and in control.

Reduces arousal, prevents panic breathing, maintains stealth on delicate or balance-dependent moves.

"Quiet in, controlled out."

🗣️Power Exhale

Firm exhale through pursed lips during a move — vocalised (grunt/scream) if it comes naturally.

Increases CO₂ clearance, narrows mental focus, boosts force output. +5 BPM adjustment per scream.

"Exhale power, don't bottle it up."

🧨Punctuated Breath

Quick boxer-style inhale/exhale bursts for 10–20 seconds during sustained high output.

Keeps VE high without slipping into uncontrolled hyperventilation.

"Punch out the breath — one move, one exhale."

🛡️Shield Breath

70–80% inhale → 0.3–0.5s hold → short pursed-lip exhale while braced.

Combines core stability with controlled CO₂ clearance — avoids a full Valsalva for sketchy or high-force moments.

"Build your shield with the breath — strong, unbreakable."

Scream-Density Override

≥ 7 screams/min or >15s combined expressive load → RCP forced, +20% W′bal depletion multiplier, diaphragm fatigue risk. 1–3 screams can enhance VO₂/focus; high density drains fast. One scream per crux, max.

🌬️Exhale Dump

Long, releasing sigh — let the shoulders drop. Full, unhurried exhale, "ahhh".

↓ sympathetic drive, speeds HR drop, clears residual CO₂ and tension. Best immediately after clipping or sending.

"Dump it out, start fresh."

🕰️HRR Reset

Slow, coherent breaths at ~5–6 breaths/min while tracking HR drop in the recovery window.

Directly stimulates parasympathetic activation, improves HRR₆₀ and readiness for the next effort.

"Reset with the rhythm of your heart."

🛶Rowing Breath

Inhale — pause — exhale — pause, like smooth oar strokes.

Enhances venous return, smooths ventilatory decline, supports lactate clearance.

"Row your breath — pull calmness back in."

🌱Grounding Breath

Slow, deep belly breath with attention on contact points — feet, hands.

Anchors attention, reduces post-crux jitters, combines physiological recovery with mental composure.

"Root into the rock — breathe through your base."

🐡Puffafish Breath

Soft nasal inhale, lightly puff the cheeks, hold 0.5–1s, then a slow, controlled exhale through pursed lips. Repeat ×1–3 only.

Reduces sympathetic tension, releases jaw/face grip, normalises CO₂ after expressive breaths. A marker of high respiratory intelligence. Do NOT hyperventilate — it's a reset, not a pump.

"Puff → pause → pour it out."

Red Limiter Pathway

HRR₆₀ < 12–18 BPM? Do HRR Reset 2–3 min + Exhale Dump between burns.

Most climbers in the redline zone show erratic or breath-hold patterns — the evenness Freja and Anna demonstrate is the anomaly. It suggests elite-level coupling between motor patterning, breath timing, and emotional regulation: controlled redline — the ability to sustain rhythmic, high-frequency breathing without losing composure or coordination.

Apply Pranaclimb To

Coach Takeaways

✅ Spot threshold zones on the wall
Quickly identify when a climber is approaching CP or RCP using simple breath and effort cues.

✅ Track fatigue and recovery in real time
Use breathing patterns and HRR₆₀ to gauge recovery between burns — no tech required.

✅ Build breath-led warm-ups and pacing
Nasal breathing, rhythm drills, and exhale control to prime the body before cruxes.

✅ Recognise inefficient breathing
Spot over-breathing, breath-holding, or gasping — and coach climbers back to control.

✅ Turn awareness into performance
Use breath as feedback — not just for effort, but recovery, pacing, and decision-making.
