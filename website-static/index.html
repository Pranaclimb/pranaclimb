The Pranaclimb Way

Methodology

A breath-led climbing performance system. Field-based, non-invasive, and built for climbing's real-world demands — dynamic movement, anaerobic bursts, expressive breathing, and micro-rests.

Climbing performance is limited as much by breathing as by strength. The Pranaclimb Methodology combines breathing physiology, breathwork training, and field-based performance metrics to help climbers improve endurance, pacing, and recovery on the wall.

📄 Published Research: Anderson, A. (2025). The Pranaclimb Methodology: Non-Invasive Tracking of Critical Power and W′bal Modelling in Rock Climbing Using Breathing Rate, RPE, and HRR. SportRxiv. DOI: 10.51224/SRXIV.578 · ResearchGate

Pranaclimb in 3 Variables

BR
Breathing Rate

Real-time effort marker. Tracks intensity as you climb — breath by breath.

RPE
CR-10 Scale

Perceived load — tightly correlated with BR and used to identify your thresholds.

HRR₆₀
Recovery Speed

Heart-rate recovery in 60 seconds. The clearest signal of how fast you bounce back.

⛰️ Critical Power (CP) ~45 BPM / RPE 8

The highest sustainable effort. Below this, W′bal recharges. Above it, the battery drains.

🔥 Resp. Compensation Point >55 BPM / RPE 9–10

Unsustainable intensity. W′bal depletes rapidly. The crux zone every climber knows.

Self-Guided Diagnostic & Training Tool for Climbers

Remote Breathing Assessment

Your journey begins with a comprehensive remote assessment — on and off the rock.

The Pranaclimb RBA is a remote, self-administered or coached assessment that uses Breathing Rate (BR), Rate of Perceived Exertion (RPE), and Heart Rate Recovery (HRR₆₀) to identify performance zones, recovery capacity, and breathing efficiency in climbing.

  • Breathing Rate (BR)
  • RPE (CR-10) — perceived effort
  • Expressive breathing (holds, grunts, screams)
  • HRR₆₀ — heart rate recovery at 60 seconds
  • Deep Dive: CO₂ tolerance, PEFR, SpO₂, rib mobility

What's measured in the real world

❓ What is the Pranaclimb Methodology?

A field-based system where breathing reveals performance. Pranaclimb non-invasively tracks climbing intensity and recovery — capturing real climbing cost that gyms and labs often miss.

  • 🫁BR — instant ventilatory effort signal
  • 🎯RPE CR-10 — climbers' internal effort scale
  • ❤️HRR₆₀ — recovery readiness between burns
  • ⚡W′bal-ODE — anaerobic reserve modelling for pacing intelligence

The Deep Dive

They test your adaptability: Power → Control → Recovery → Calm → Flexibility.

  • 💨PEFR — strong exhalation = delay metabolic shutdown
  • 🌬️CO₂ Tolerance (MBT) — stay composed when CO₂ rises
  • 🩸SpO₂ — faster O₂ recovery = faster W′bal recharge
  • 🧘NRP — sigh resets + nasal rhythm improve HRR₆₀
  • 🏗️BIQ — free diaphragm = efficient breathing economy

👉 These five pillars show how well you cope when climbing gets real.

The Person Behind It

Annie Anderson

Founder of the Pranaclimb Methodology

A breath-led climbing performance system · Author of Pranaclimb: Love, Breathe, Climb

Annie Anderson is an independent researcher, breathwork specialist, and lifelong climber with over 30 years of worldwide experience in climbing, yoga, and performance science. She began Trad climbing in 1991 on the Avon Gorge limestone in Bristol, and from 1998 to 2001 managed the UAE's first climbing wall in Dubai — creating and running courses from scratch.

She is the creator of the Pranaclimb Methodology — a field-adapted system that uses breathing rate (BR), perceived exertion (RPE), and heart-rate recovery (HRR₆₀) to track performance thresholds and recovery in real-time. Her work bridges sports physiology and practical coaching, helping climbers harness breath as both a performance tool and a pathway to resilience. Her research has been published on SportRxiv and ResearchGate, with ongoing submissions to peer-reviewed journals.

Annie lives with her husband and daughter beside the sea cliffs of Cornwall, where she climbs, swims daily in the icy Atlantic, and practises breathwork and yoga year-round. Her philosophy — live, love life every day — carried her through three years of Stage 4 Renal Cell Carcinoma and surgery. Family, friends, the ocean, climbing, breathwork, cold water, positivity, and gratitude continue to inspire every moment.

"Breathing is the one system that crosses the boundary between conscious and unconscious. That's extraordinary — and completely untapped by most climbers."
Qualified Climbing Instructor Yoga Teacher RYT 200 Advanced First Aider Breathwork Specialist Respiratory Physiology Researcher Cold Water Coach Stage 4 Cancer Survivor 30+ Years Climbing

Her upcoming book Pranaclimb: Love, Breathe, Climb combines science, breathwork, and elite insights, offering unique practices for climbers.
