RP & RP Sync
in the Wild

Readiness Potential. RP Sync. The neuroscience of timing breath to movement at the critical moment. Will Bosi on Burden of Dreams V17 and Jimmy Webb on Sleepwalker V16 — analysed second by second using the Pranaclimb framework.

Annie Anderson
Researcher · Breathwork Specialist · Climber
By Annie Anderson · Pranaclimb 7 min read

🧠 What is RP — Readiness Potential?

The Readiness Potential (RP) is a measurable neural signal that appears in the brain 500–2000ms before a voluntary movement. First identified by Kornhuber & Deecke (1965), it represents the brain's preparation for action — the neurological build-up of motor intent before the body moves.

In climbing, RP is the invisible moment of commitment before the hardest move. It's what happens in the stillness just before you go. The exhale, the breath hold, the settling — these are the external expression of the Readiness Potential building in the nervous system.

RP in climbing: Silent, neural, often invisible — but energetically real. It precedes movement, not accompanies it. The breath before the move is as important as the breath during it.

⚡ What is RP Sync?

RP Sync is the Pranaclimb term for the precise timing of an exhale to coincide with the hardest phase of a movement — usually the moment of maximum force application. It is the expression of the Readiness Potential in breath.

🧠 RP — Readiness Potential

  • → Neural preparation phase
  • → Occurs before the move
  • → Silent — often an exhale or settling breath
  • → Loads motor intent and focus
  • → Invisible but energetically present
  • → Will Bosi 16:33 — the exhale before

⚡ RP Sync — Execution

  • → Breath timed to movement
  • → Occurs during the move
  • → Exhale timed to force application
  • → Maximises force, minimises metabolic waste
  • → The exhale that lands the hold
  • → Will Bosi 16:35 — the exhale on execution
"You're not just breathing — you're speaking the nervous system's language. RP is the question. RP Sync is the answer."

🧗 Will Bosi — Burden of Dreams V17

Burden of Dreams is the hardest boulder problem in the world. Watch the send at timestamp 16:33 — three seconds that contain a near-perfect RP → RP Sync sequence:

16:33
💨 Exhale RP — controlled release, parasympathetic reset, diaphragm drops. Intent loading. This is the RP — neural preparation before the move.
16:34
🫁 Deep inhale — diaphragm priming, O₂ loading, trunk pre-tension. Postural expansion for motor priming.
16:35
⚡ RP Sync Exhale — exhale timed precisely to the hardest move. Maximum force with minimum metabolic waste. Textbook RP Sync.
Pranaclimb insight: This is RP without chaos — no scream, no stacking, no wasted metabolic cost. Will Bosi demonstrates timing over intensity. The exhale at 16:33 is not about ventilation — it marks intent, focus, and neural loading. The final exhale (RP Sync) coincides precisely with commitment and force application. A masterclass in efficiency.

🔍 Key Pranaclimb Takeaways

🧠 RP = Preparation

Silent, neural, often invisible — but energetically real. The exhale before the move signals intent, not just ventilation. Bosi's 16:33 is the RP.

⚡ RP Sync = Execution

The exhale that lands the move. Timed to maximum force application. Bosi's 16:35 and Webb's 5:52 are both textbook RP Sync.

🪨 Bouldering Hides Cost

Breath holds and screams mask true ventilatory demand. Sleepwalker's adjusted BR of 55–60 BPM puts it firmly in RCP zone — this is a route, not "just" a boulder problem.

🎉 YESSSS is Physiology

The emotional release at 6:07 and WOOOHOOOO at 6:12 are not just celebration. They are relief sighs — vagal activation, parasympathetic reset, HRR₆₀ beginning.

⏱️ Sequencing Over Force

Sleepwalker shows how breath sequencing — BH → nasal inhale → RP Sync exhale — allows climbers to survive near-total W′bal depletion. Timing matters more than raw power.

🧗 Even Boulders Cross CP

Long boulder sequences (~40s+) push into CP/RCP zones. The physiological demands are closer to a short sport route than a single explosive move.

"Sequencing matters more than force. Sleepwalker shows how breath timing allows climbers to survive near-total W′bal depletion."

🫁 How to Train RP Sync

RP Sync is trainable. Start on easy terrain where you can focus purely on breath timing — not on not falling:

  • 1On easy routes — consciously exhale as you move through each hard hold. Notice how it affects power and composure.
  • 2At cruxes on your project — identify the single hardest move. Practise the RP exhale → inhale → RP Sync exhale sequence in isolation.
  • 3Use verbal cues — "exhale and commit" or "breathe and move" timed to the crux. The cue anchors the timing.
  • 4Video review — watch your own clips and listen for the breath. Are you holding? Exhaling before or after the move? This is exactly what the Pranaclimb RBA captures.
