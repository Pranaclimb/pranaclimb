2.5 Billion Years Ago
The Most Important Evolutionary Leap
Bluish-green cyanobacteria emerged in the Archaean ocean and transformed Earth's atmosphere. This was the beginning of photosynthesis. These tiny organisms absorbed light from the sun and used it to make sugars from carbon dioxide and water. The waste produced was oxygen — and life began to evolve on Earth.
Today
Prochlorococcus: Earth's Smallest Oxygen Factory
Prochlorococcus is the smallest photosynthetic organism on Earth. But this little bacteria produces up to 20% of the oxygen in our entire biosphere. Every fifth breath you take — thank phytoplankton.
