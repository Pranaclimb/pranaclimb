📋 Risk Assessment & Management

Every Ocean Dips session includes a comprehensive safety briefing and full risk assessment before entering the water.

Before Every Session

  • Full weather and tide check for the day
  • Assessment of entry and exit points at each location
  • Hazard assessment — swell, currents, sea bed, wind direction
  • Water temperature check
  • Safety briefing for all participants
  • Review of any medical conditions or concerns disclosed by participants

During Every Session

  • Slow, controlled entry — no jumping
  • Breath control guided before swimming
  • Awareness of cold shock response in all participants
  • Time limits based on conditions and individual tolerance
  • Continuous monitoring throughout the dip
  • Annie remains on the water's edge or in the water at all times

Environmental Considerations

  • Afterdrop — continued cooling after exiting the water
  • Wind chill factor and its effect on cooling
  • Individual cold tolerance (varies day to day)
  • Tidal conditions — sessions timed around safe tide windows
  • Water quality — sessions only run in clean, safe water

⚠️ If in doubt, we don't go out. All Ocean Dips sessions are weather and tide dependent. If conditions are not safe, sessions will be rescheduled. Your safety always comes first.

🌊 Key Safety Tips

  • Know your entry and exit points before you get in
  • Never swim alone — always go with a guide or buddy
  • Assess the hazards before entering the water
  • If it looks dangerous or you feel out of your comfort zone — don't go in
  • Know which way the wind is blowing and the direction of any current
  • Swim at tidal pools at low tide. Swim at beaches at high tide, or within one hour of high tide
  • There is no universal time limit — every body is different. Listen to yours
  • Start with small dips and extend gradually over time
  • Never jump into cold water — always enter slowly and in a controlled way
  • Keep a mobile phone accessible at all times
Annie carries a first aid kit and waterproof mobile phones at all sessions. Every experience includes a full safety briefing tailored to the location and conditions of the day.

🏥 Medical Conditions

Many people with minor medical conditions can take part in Ocean Dips safely, provided they disclose these before their session.

  • Conditions like mild asthma, mild diabetes, or controlled blood pressure rarely prevent participation
  • Any required medication can be carried in our waterproof safety bag
  • Please inform Annie in advance of any health conditions so she can advise appropriately

⚠️ Always consult your GP first if you have a heart condition, high blood pressure, severe asthma, or are pregnant. Cold water can place significant demands on the cardiovascular system.

If in doubt, always consult your GP before participating. Annie is happy to discuss any concerns before you book.
