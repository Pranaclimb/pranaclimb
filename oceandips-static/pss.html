Perceived Stress Scale

A research-validated tool used at the start of each Ocean Dips session. Understand your stress levels before — and discover the difference after. 🌊

What is the PSS?
The Perceived Stress Scale (PSS) is one of the most widely used psychological tools for measuring the perception of stress. Developed by Dr Sheldon Cohen at Carnegie Mellon University, it helps us understand how different situations affect your feelings and perceived stress.

At Ocean Dips, Annie uses this scale at the beginning of each session as a gentle check-in — and encourages guests to retake it afterwards to witness the measurable impact of cold water, breathwork, and time in nature on their wellbeing. 🌊
