🌊 Ocean Dips — Wild Swimming FAQ

Cold water has a powerful effect on both body and mind. It can help:

• Reduce soreness and support recovery
• Increase energy and alertness
• Improve mood and resilience

But the real magic is in the breath. When you learn to control your breathing in cold water, you learn to stay calm in discomfort — a skill that carries into everyday life. 🌊
The key to cold water is your breathing. When you enter cold water, your body experiences a cold shock response:

• Gasp reflex
• Rapid breathing
• Increased heart rate

Through guided breathing, you learn to:

• Control the exhale
• Slow the breath
• Regulate your nervous system
• Stay calm in the cold

Your experience is guided with a strong focus on safety:

• Slow, controlled entry (no jumping)
• Breath control before swimming
• Awareness of cold shock response
• Time limits based on conditions
• Structured warm-up after

We also consider:

• Afterdrop (continued cooling after exit)
• Wind chill
• Individual tolerance

You will be guided throughout. 🌊

All Ocean Dips experiences take place on the wild North Cornish coast of West Cornwall, starting from Gwithian near Hayle. Locations include Gwithian, Godrevy, Porthmeor Cove, Land's End, and Nanjizal. The exact meeting point is confirmed after booking and depends on tide, swell and wave height.
All sessions are weather and tide dependent. Annie monitors conditions closely and will contact you ahead of your session with any updates. In the event of unsafe conditions your session will be rescheduled at no extra cost. If rescheduling is not possible a full refund will be offered.
Please disclose any medical conditions, injuries or health concerns at the time of booking. Many conditions can be accommodated safely — Annie carries medication in a waterproof safety bag. Heart conditions, severe asthma, high blood pressure or pregnancy — please seek GP advice first. If in doubt, always consult your doctor before booking.
Please do not book if any of the following apply without first consulting your GP:

❌ Heart conditions or cardiovascular disease
❌ High blood pressure (uncontrolled)
❌ Severe or uncontrolled asthma
❌ Epilepsy
❌ Raynaud's syndrome (severe)
❌ Recent surgery or injury
❌ Pregnancy
❌ Any condition affected by cold water or sudden temperature change

Cold water immersion causes an immediate physiological response — your heart rate and blood pressure rise rapidly. For most people this is safe and exhilarating, but for some conditions it can be dangerous. If in doubt, always consult your GP before booking.
